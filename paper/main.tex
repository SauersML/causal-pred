\begin{abstract}
We present Causal-Pred, an end-to-end Bayesian pipeline that combines
Mendelian-randomisation-informed structure learning (MrDAG,
\citealt{Zuber2025}) with a warm-starting hill climber
(DAGSLAM, \citealt{Zhao2025DAGSLAM}), structure MCMC with a
Giudici--Castelo neighbourhood correction \citep{GiudiciCastelo2003}, and a
distributional survival GAM \citep{RigbyStasinopoulos2005, Wood2011}.
Applied to an All of Us--shaped synthetic cohort of \nSamples{} subjects
over \nNodes{} nodes, the pipeline recovers \edgeRecall{} of known causal
edges, attains a time-dependent AUC of \aurocTen{} at 10 years
\citep{Heagerty2000, Uno2007}, a Brier score of \brier{} with an
integrated Brier score of \ibs{} \citep{Brier1950, Graf1999, Murphy1973},
a Nagelkerke $R^2$ of \nagelkerke{} \citep{Nagelkerke1991}, and an
expected calibration error of \ece{} \citep{Naeini2015,
HosmerLemeshow1980}. Runtime is \runtimeSeconds{} seconds on a workstation
and the full structure MCMC mixed at $\hat{R} = \mcmcRhat{}$ across
\mcmcChains{} chains.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Individual-level disease-risk prediction on biobank-scale data is usually
cast as a supervised learning problem with no explicit causal model and
no structural uncertainty. Two recent lines of work change the game.
Mendelian randomisation exploits genetic instruments as natural
experiments to estimate causal effects between exposures
\citep{DaveySmith2014, Pearl2009}, and Bayesian structure learning over
DAGs propagates posterior uncertainty over graphs rather than committing
to a single structure \citep{MadiganYork1995, Kuipers2014}. We combine
the two by using MR summary statistics as a prior on edge inclusion
\citep{Zuber2025}, warm-starting structure MCMC with DAGSLAM
\citep{Zhao2025DAGSLAM}, and then composing the posterior parent sets
with a distributional survival GAM to produce individual-level survival
curves with both structural and parametric uncertainty.

\section{Background}

Mendelian randomisation uses germline genotypes -- randomised at meiosis --
as instrumental variables to estimate causal effects between measured
exposures \citep{DaveySmith2014, Corbin2016}. For metabolic-disease
outcomes, well-replicated examples include BMI $\to$ type 2 diabetes
\citep{Fall2015, Xue2018}, LDL $\to$ coronary disease
\citep{Ference2017, Emdin2017}, blood pressure $\to$ cardiovascular
outcomes \citep{Nazarzadeh2022}, and null/negative findings for alcohol
intake \citep{Wootton2020}. Summary-data IVW estimates \citep{Glover1989}
are straightforward to aggregate across thousands of variants and form
the raw input to our structural prior.

Bayesian structure learning over DAGs scores each candidate graph by a
marginal likelihood with a conjugate structure prior
\citep{Geiger2002, Kuipers2014} and explores the space via Markov chain
Monte Carlo with neighbourhood-balanced proposals
\citep{GiudiciCastelo2003, MadiganYork1995}. Bayesian model averaging
over the posterior propagates structural uncertainty into downstream
predictions \citep{Draper1995}.

\section{Methods}

\subsection{Data}

The target disease is type 2 diabetes. The synthetic generator produces
a cohort of $n = \nSamples{}$ subjects on $p = \nNodes{}$ nodes with
node-level event rate \eventRate{}, matching the column convention of
All of Us \citep{AllofUs2024} and UK Biobank \citep{Bycroft2018}. Node
types split into continuous covariates (BMI, LDL, HbA1c, blood
pressure), binary lifestyle indicators, and a survival outcome. Each
subject carries a polygenic score computed externally via the
\texttt{gnomon} CLI \citep{Khera2018}.

\subsection{MrDAG: edge-inclusion prior from MR summary statistics}

Following \citet{Zuber2025} we place a spike-and-slab prior on each
directed edge $j \to k$ in the DAG. Conditional on edge inclusion the
causal effect is Gaussian, $\theta_{jk} \mid \gamma_{jk} = 1 \sim
\mathcal{N}(0, \tau^2)$; conditional on exclusion it is concentrated on
a practical null, $\theta_{jk} \mid \gamma_{jk} = 0 \sim
\mathcal{N}(0, \omega^2)$ with $\omega \ll \tau$ \citep{Wakefield2009}.
Given the IVW summary $(\hat\beta_{jk}, \hat\sigma_{jk})$ the per-edge
Bayes factor is closed form and the posterior edge-inclusion probability
collapses to
\[
\pi_{jk} = \frac{\mathrm{BF}_{jk}\, p_{\text{prior}}}
               {\mathrm{BF}_{jk}\, p_{\text{prior}} + (1 - p_{\text{prior}})}.
\]

\subsection{BGe + Laplace mixed-type scoring}

Continuous nodes are scored by the BGe marginal likelihood with a
Wishart prior \citep{Geiger2002, Kuipers2014}. Binary nodes are scored
by a Laplace approximation to the marginal likelihood of a ridge-logistic
regression on the parent set. Survival nodes delegate to the distributional
GAM of \S3.6.

\subsection{DAGSLAM warm start}

DAGSLAM \citep{Zhao2025DAGSLAM} performs greedy edge addition, deletion
and reversal, evaluating at each step
$\Delta = \Delta_{\text{score}} + \Delta_{\text{prior}}$
and accepting the best-scoring move subject to acyclicity. The output is
the warm-start DAG $G_0$ consumed by the structure MCMC.

\subsection{Structure MCMC with Giudici--Castelo correction}

We run $\mcmcChains{}$ parallel chains of $\mcmcIter{}$ iterations each,
starting from $G_0$. Each proposal is an edge addition, deletion, or
reversal with the Giudici--Castelo neighbourhood correction
\citep{GiudiciCastelo2003, MadiganYork1995}:
\[
\alpha(G \to G') = \min\!\left(1,\;
\frac{s(G')\, p(G')\, |N(G)|}
     {s(G)\, p(G)\, |N(G')|}\right),
\]
with per-node score caching. Mean acceptance was \mcmcAccept{} and
$\hat{R}_{\max} = \mcmcRhat{}$.

\subsection{Distributional survival GAM}

The survival time $T \mid x$ is modelled distributionally
\citep{RigbyStasinopoulos2005} with log-location and log-scale each a
sum of P-spline expansions \citep{EilersMarx1996}. REML selects the
smoothing penalties \citep{Wood2011} and NUTS
\citep{HoffmanGelman2014} samples the posterior over spline
coefficients.

\subsection{Bayesian model averaging}

Posterior predictive survival curves are averaged across the visited DAG
posterior \citep{MadiganYork1995, Draper1995}. For a functional $f$,
$\E[f \mid \mathrm{data}] = \sum_{G} w_G\, \E[f \mid G, \mathrm{data}]$,
with weights $w_G$ equal to MCMC visitation frequency.

\subsection{Validation}

We report edge-recall against the synthetic ground-truth DAG, edge-level
AUROC of the posterior edge-inclusion matrix, individual-level AUROC,
time-dependent AUC at 5, 10, 15 years \citep{Heagerty2000, Uno2007},
Brier score with the Murphy decomposition \citep{Murphy1973, Brier1950,
Graf1999}, Nagelkerke $R^2$ \citep{Nagelkerke1991}, and expected
calibration error \citep{Naeini2015, HosmerLemeshow1980}.

\section{Discussion}

\paragraph{Limitations.}
All of Us individual-level data are only accessible inside the
Researcher Workbench \citep{AllofUs2024}; the reported numbers are
therefore on a synthetic biobank-shape cohort with a known ground-truth
DAG. On real data, HbA1c is used both as a risk factor and, implicitly,
in the adjudication of type 2 diabetes, which gives rise to a mild
circularity; we flag this as a caveat and recommend using a
pre-adjudication baseline HbA1c window in any real-data deployment.

\paragraph{Prior work on causal priors.}
Our edge-inclusion prior is informed by published Mendelian-randomisation
evidence (e.g.\ \citealt{Fall2015, Xue2018, Ference2017}). Where no
published IVW estimate is available, the prior is left non-informative
rather than imputed, so the structural posterior is never biased by
unattributed prior claims \citep{DaveySmith2014}.

\section{Reproducibility}

All code is pinned via \texttt{uv.lock}. The GAM backend is the
Rust-based \texttt{SauersML/gam} package built through \texttt{rustup}.
Polygenic scoring, HWE-PCA projection, and sample-term inference shell
out to the \texttt{gnomon} CLI. Every stage accepts an explicit RNG and
derives child seeds deterministically. A full run is reproduced by
\texttt{uv run python scripts/run\_full\_pipeline.py} followed by
\texttt{make -C paper paper}.
